A 16-year-old from Jaipur.
One book changed everything.

BookVyrse wasn't born in a boardroom. It was born in a bedroom — in the mind of a girl who spent years refusing to read self-help books until one day, she couldn't stop.

01
The Stubborn Daughter
My dad tried to get me to read self-help books for years. I refused every single time.

My father is a reader. Not the fiction kind — the self-help, underline-every-page, tell-your-daughter-about-it-at-dinner kind. For years he would slide books across the table. The 7 Habits. Rich Dad Poor Dad. Atomic Habits. Each one with a quiet "beta, just try this one."

And for years, I — the stubborn, fictional-novels-only kind of daughter — said no. I was the kid who lived in fantasy worlds. Self-help felt fake to me. Too polished. Too much like being told what to do.

"I genuinely thought self-help books were for people who didn't have a personality yet. I was 15 and very wrong about that."— Keya
02
The Book That Broke Me Open
Then I read one book. And everything I thought I knew cracked right down the middle.

I picked up Don't Believe Everything You Think. Something was different. The language was plain. It asked questions instead of giving orders. For the first time, a book felt like it was talking directly to me.

Then Atomic Habits. And that's when the real problem hit me: we can all read a book. The part where we fall apart is actually using it. The implementation. The making-it-real. Nobody teaches you that.

"The problem was never the books. The problem is that nothing helps you actually use what you read."— The insight that started BookVyrse
03
It Runs in the Family
My mother is a founder. I grew up watching someone build something from nothing.

Growing up in that house meant growing up around a certain kind of energy — the late nights, the pivots, the conversations about customers and problems and what actually needs to exist in the world. The startup mindset wasn't something I had to learn. It was just the air I breathed at home.

Watching my mother build something from nothing taught me one thing before anything else: you don't wait until you're ready. You start, and you figure it out as you go.

04
The Dad Moment
The same man who gave me books for years called me at night and said "Keya, this is going to rock."

When I told my dad about BookVyrse, he was supportive — but cautiously. He's a practical man. Then one night — maybe a week later — he called me. He'd been re-reading Deep Work by Cal Newport. The ideas weren't clicking the way they used to.

"Keya, this is exactly what BookVyrse needs to solve. I've read this book — but I need something to help me actually use it. This idea is going to rock."— Keya's father, the original BookVyrse believer

That phone call meant more than any investor pitch could. The man who had been handing me books for years was telling me that even he needed what I was building.

05
Why This Matters
I'm 16. I started this with zero connections and one book. Ideas don't wait for the right age.

I was that student. Surrounded by self-help books I wouldn't touch. Friends who bought them and abandoned them. A culture that says "just read more" without explaining how to make reading matter.

BookVyrse is my answer. Not a summary app. A real, AI-powered companion that meets you where you are, gives you a path, challenges you to apply what you've learned, and never lets you feel alone with a book again.
