✦ The real story behind BookVyrse

A 16-year-old from Jaipur.
A stubborn daughter.
One book that changed everything.

BookVyrse wasn't born in a boardroom. It wasn't born in a college hackathon. It was born in a bedroom in Jaipur, in the mind of a girl who spent years refusing to read self-help books — until one day, she couldn't stop.

K
Keya — Founder, BookVyrse 16 years old · Jaipur, Rajasthan
01
The stubborn daughter

My dad tried to get me to read self-help books for years. I refused every single time.

My father is a reader. Not the fiction kind — the self-help, underline-every-page, tell-your-daughter-about-it-at-dinner kind. For years he would slide books across the table. The 7 Habits. Rich Dad Poor Dad. Atomic Habits. Each one with a quiet "beta, just try this one."

And for years, I — the stubborn, fictional-novels-only, absolutely-not kind of daughter — said no. I was the kid who lived in fantasy worlds, in stories where things happened to people. Self-help felt fake to me. Too polished. Too preachy. Too much like being told what to do.

"I genuinely thought self-help books were for people who didn't have a personality yet. I was 15 and very wrong about that." — Keya

My friends felt the same. Every time someone at school mentioned finishing a self-help book, we'd all silently know — they started it, read two chapters, and gave up. Nobody actually finished them. Nobody actually understood them. And nobody talked about why.

02
The book that broke me open

Then I read one book. And everything I thought I knew cracked right down the middle.

I don't even fully remember how it ended up in my hands. But I picked up Don't Believe Everything You Think — and something about the language was different. It wasn't commanding. It wasn't telling me I was broken and here's how to fix it. It was just… talking to me. In plain words. Asking questions I'd never thought to ask myself.

💡

It taught me to counter the thoughts I'd always accepted as true. The ones people around me had put there. The ones I'd never questioned. And that feeling — of seeing your own mind differently for the first time — I wanted every student to feel that.

Then I read Atomic Habits. And that's when the real problem hit me square in the face: we can all read a book. That's the easy part. The part where we fall apart is actually using it. The implementation. The making-it-real. Nobody teaches you that. No book does it for you. You're just supposed to figure it out alone.

Most people don't. Most people close the book, put it on a shelf, and quietly feel like they failed.

03
It runs in the family

I didn't know anything about markets, funding, or startups. But I'd grown up watching someone build one.

My mother is a founder. And growing up in that house meant growing up around a certain kind of energy — the late nights, the pivots, the conversations about customers and problems and what actually needs to exist in the world. I absorbed all of it without even realising it. The startup mindset wasn't something I had to learn. It was just the air I breathed at home.

👩‍💼

Watching my mother build something from nothing taught me one thing before anything else: you don't wait until you're ready. You start, and you figure it out as you go. That's the only way anything real ever gets built.

So when the BookVyrse idea hit me, I wasn't scared of not knowing enough. I didn't do market research. I didn't write a business plan. I didn't wait. I just thought: students like me are suffering because reading feels impossible and no one helps them implement what they learn. That was enough. That was the whole brief.

What happened next — in one day

I sat down and started brainstorming. No clue where to start, so I just picked a starting point — the landing page. I built it. One day. Then I worked on the logo. Then the website. Then the AI layer. One thing at a time, no plan, just momentum. I kept telling myself: it doesn't matter where you start. Just start.

The whole thing moved fast because I wasn't waiting to be ready. I was 16 and building something I genuinely believed the world needed. That felt like enough permission.

04
The dad moment

The same man who gave me books for years called me at night and said "Keya, this is going to rock."

When I told my dad about BookVyrse, he was supportive — but cautiously. He's a practical man. He loves books but he also knows how hard building anything real actually is. He heard me out. He nodded. He didn't shoot it down. But I could tell he was reserving judgment.

Then one night — maybe a week later — he called me. He'd been reading Deep Work by Cal Newport. He'd read it before, years ago. But the ideas weren't clicking the way they used to. He couldn't figure out why things that once felt clear now felt fuzzy.

"Keya," he said, "this is exactly what BookVyrse needs to solve. I've read this book — but I need something to help me actually use it. This idea is going to rock." — Keya's father, the original BookVyrse believer

That phone call meant more than any investor pitch could. Because it came from the man who'd been handing me books for years — and he was telling me that even he needed what I was building. If a lifelong reader needed help turning books into action, then every student in India definitely did.

05
Why this matters

I'm not building BookVyrse because I love books. I'm building it because I know what it feels like to be the student who doesn't.

I was that student. Surrounded by self-help books I wouldn't touch. Friends who bought them and abandoned them. A culture that says "just read more" without ever explaining how to make reading matter.

BookVyrse is my answer to all of it. Not a summary app. Not a list of quotes. A real, AI-powered companion that meets you where you are, gives you a path, challenges you to apply what you've learned, and never lets you feel alone with a book again.

🚀

I'm 16. I'm from Jaipur. I started this with zero connections, zero funding, and one book that changed how I see my own mind. If that's not proof that ideas don't wait for the right age — I don't know what is.

The students I'm building for are exactly like who I was six months ago: smart, curious, a little lost, and looking for something that actually helps. BookVyrse is that something. And we're only just getting started.

This is Keya's story. What's yours?

Common Questions

Frequently asked

Is BookVyrse free to use?▾
Yes! BookVyrse starts completely free. You get book previews, key lessons, and limited AI questions on the website. The full app experience — unlimited AI, full reading paths, and progress tracking — will have a generous free tier.
Who is BookVyrse for?▾
Students from Class 9 through postgraduates, and anyone aged 13–25 who wants to grow. If you've ever started a book and not finished it, or wanted a mentor but couldn't afford one, BookVyrse is exactly for you.
How is this different from a summary app?▾
Summary apps give you bullet points. BookVyrse gives you a mentor. We curate your path, guide your reading, set real challenges, and track your growth. The goal isn't to replace reading — it's to make your reading actually change your life.
